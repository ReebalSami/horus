Kerr 1998 — *Personality and Social Psychology Review* 2(3), 196–217. Foundational methodological-rigour paper coining the acronym **HARKing** (Hypothesizing After the Results are Known): the practice of presenting post-hoc hypotheses as if they had been the original a-priori predictions. Kerr lays out 12 distinct concerns about HARKing — concealment of true exploratory analyses, inflated apparent confirmation rates, theory-protection biases, etc. Cited in HORUS as the **methodological reference for §4.1** of brainstorm v2 (locked-a-priori commitments) — the discipline of fixing what's locked before seeing experimental results, and of explicitly distinguishing locked hypotheses from open-search regions in the experiment phase. Anchors the thesis's scientific-correctness commitment that drives ADR-001 (full ADR per tool/model decision with `Supersession trigger` pre-commitments).
