Cai & O'Connor 2025 (Applied Network Science / arXiv 2506.12367). First evaluation of KG-extraction quality at TWO levels simultaneously: (1) **micro-level edge accuracy** consistent with NLP-extraction-eval norms, and (2) **macro-level graph-structural properties** (community detection, connectivity, structural metrics). Demonstrates that extraction-quality decline produces *systematic* biases in graph-level metrics — not just additive noise. Cited in HORUS as the **direct prior for the thesis novelty** (per brainstorm v2 §7.4): HORUS extends Cai & O'Connor's work along three axes: (a) image→KG instead of their text→KG; (b) compliance-aware fidelity metric instead of generic structural metrics; (c) Layer-3 query-accuracy propagation (how does extraction quality flow into downstream QA accuracy?). The clearest single-paper anchor for the thesis's methodological contribution.
